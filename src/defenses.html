Allowable Defenses

You have seven (7) days from the date of issuance of a parking or compliance violation to contest the ticket. If neither a contest or payment is received, a second Notice of Violation is mailed allowing twenty-one (21) days to request a hearing.

Parking/Compliance Violations

  • You didn’t own the car at the time, or you had leased your car to someone else, and provided the name and address of that person within 60 days of being notified, following these rules in the city code.
  • The car that was ticketed was stolen or had stolen plates.
  • The signs with parking regulations were covered or missing.
  • The parking meter was not working properly.
  • Based on the facts stated in the violation, you didn’t actually break the rule you are accused of breaking. For example, you were ticketed for not having a city sticker, but you’ve lived in Chicago for less that 30 days (or purchased the car less than 30 days before). Or, your tinted windows comply with the medical use requirement.
  • The facts in the violation you received are not true. For example, your car was not parked illegally when the violation was issued.
  • You have fixed the compliance issue you were ticketed for. There are exceptions: you cannot argue this if your compliance issue was for a city sticker (section of city code), an exhaust problem violating part (a)(2) of this section, registration plate problems violating part (a)(2) or (f) of this section, or glass coverings/coatings violating this section.

You have twenty-one (21) days from the Notice of Violation date to contest the issuance of an automated traffic law enforcement (i.e. red light) or automated speed camera enforcement violation.

Red Light Violations

  • You were already issued a citation for a red light violation (part 3) at the time.
  • The car that was ticketed was stolen or had stolen plates (the theft has to have been reported at the time).
  • Your car was an authorized emergency vehicle or was participating in a funeral procession.
  • The facts stated in the violation are wrong, or don’t show that you broke the city code.
  • You didn’t own the car at the time, or you had leased your car to someone else, and provided the name and address of that person within 120 days of being notified, following these rules in the city code.

Speeding Violations

  • You were already issued a speeding ticket within ⅛ of a mile and 15 minutes of the one that was automatically recorded.
  • The facts stated in the violation are wrong or don’t show that you violated any of these sections of the municipal code:
    • 9-12-070 Speed limits
    • 9-12-075 School safety zones
    • 9-12-077 Park and playground safety zones
  • The car that was ticketed was stolen or had stolen plates (the theft has to have been reported at the time).
  • You had leased your car to someone else, and provided the name and address of that person within 120 days of being notified, following these rules in the city code.
  • Your vehicle was an authorized emergency vehicle.
  • You did not own or lease the vehicle at the time.

Bus Lane Violations

  • You were already issued a ticket for one of these violations:
    • 9-12-060 Bus lanes. Part b
    • 9-64-140 Common-carrier vehicle stops and stands. Part b
  • The car that was ticketed was stolen or had stolen plates (the theft has to have been reported at the time).
  • You had leased your car to someone else, and provided the name and address of that person within 120 days of being notified, following these rules in the city code.
  • Your car is an authorized emergency vehicle.
  • The facts stated in the violation are wrong or don’t show that you violated these bus lane/stop rules:
    • 9-12-060 Bus lanes. Part b
    • 9-64-140 Common-carrier vehicle stops and stands. Part b
  • You didn’t own or lease the vehicle at the time.
  • You were making a right hand turn.
  • Your vehicle was not a licensed taxicab: you were actively loading or unloading passengers, and you were not interfering with any bus trying to enter the bus lane.
  • Your vehicle was a licensed taxicab: you were actively loading or unloading passengers.
